\begin{abstract}
The \emph{Square Peg Problem}, introduced by \emph{Otto Toeplitz} in 1911, asks whether every simple closed Jordan curve $C$ inscribes a squareâ€”that is, whether four points on $C$ can form the vertices of a square. To approach this, we introduce the notion of \emph{$4\text{-}\Delta$} sets and rotations, as a special case of the more general $^n\Delta_k$ configurations, defined as sets of $k$ lines in $n$-dimensional space with equal mutual angles.

For $n = 2$ and $k = 4$, this configuration consists of two perpendicular line pairs (angle $\pi/2$). We prove that the continuous rotation of such a configuration over a Jordan curve causes the vertical and horizontal line pairs to swap positions. By the \emph{Intermediate Value Theorem}, this implies the existence of a moment when vertical and horizontal lines coincide in length, thus forming the diagonals of an inscribed square.
\end{abstract}
